Russia-Ukraine War

An ongoing and protracted conflict that began in February 2014, escalating into a full-scale invasion in February 2022.

Conflict History

The conflict originated in 2014 following the Ukrainian Revolution of Dignity, focusing on the status of Crimea and parts of the Donbas, internationally recognized as part of Ukraine. After years of simmering conflict, Russia launched a full-scale invasion, marking a major escalation and creating Europe's largest refugee crisis since World War II. The invasion has been met with widespread international condemnation, sanctions against Russia, and significant military assistance for Ukraine.

Current Situation

As of late 2024, the conflict is characterized by a war of attrition along a vast front line in eastern and southern Ukraine. Both sides are heavily reliant on artillery, drone warfare, and fortified defensive positions. Key strategic objectives include control over critical infrastructure and logistical hubs. The international community continues to play a pivotal role, with ongoing debates about the scale and nature of military and financial support for Ukraine's defense efforts.
